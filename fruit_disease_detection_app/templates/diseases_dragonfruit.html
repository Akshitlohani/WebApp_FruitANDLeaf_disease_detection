Dragon Fruit Diseases


Learn about various Dragon fruit diseases that can affect your crop. Know about the symptoms, root causes, favorable conditions and measures to prevent the disease.


1. Anthracnose

Disease symptoms:
  • Circular, sunken lesions on the fruit, often with pink or orange spore masses in the center. Lesions can start small and expand, leading to severe rotting.

Root Causes:
  • The fungus can survive on plant debris, in the soil, or on alternate hosts. Spores spread through rain splash, wind, insects, or contaminated tools.

Favourable Conditions:
  • Warm, humid weather.
  • Overhead irrigation that splashes water on the fruit and foliage
  • Wounds on the plant from insect damage or pruning

Preventive Measures:
  • Sanitation:Regularly remove and destroy fallen fruits and plant debris to reduce the source of inoculum.
  • Pruning:Prune plants to improve air circulation and reduce humidity around the fruit and foliage.
  • Water Management: Avoid overhead irrigation. Use drip irrigation to minimize water splash on fruit and foliage.
  • Chemical Control: Apply fungicides such as chlorothalonil, mancozeb, or copper-based products as a preventive measure, especially during warm and humid conditions.
  • Weather Monitoring: Be vigilant during periods of warm, humid weather, and take preventive actions such as timely fungicide applications and improved sanitation.
  • Integrated Pest Management (IPM): Monitor for insect pests that can cause wounds and take appropriate measures to control them to reduce entry points for the fungus.

2. Bacterial Soft Rot

Disease symptoms:
  • Rotting can extend deep into the center of the fruit and stem, often resulting in a hollow shell.

Root Causes:
  • Caused by various bacterial species, including Erwinia and Pectobacterium species.

Favourable Conditions:
  • High humidity and warm temperatures.
  • Wet conditions, especially following rain or overhead irrigation.
  • Injuries or wounds on the plant.

Preventive Measures:
  • Sanitation: Disinfect tools and equipment regularly to avoid cross-contamination.
  • Water Management: Avoid overhead irrigation to prevent water from splashing onto the fruit and foliage.
  • Pruning: Prune to improve air circulation and sunlight penetration, which can help reduce humidity around the plants.
  • Chemical Control: Use bactericides like copper-based sprays or other recommended bactericidal treatments as a preventive measure.
  • Wound Management: Avoid unnecessary wounding of plants during pruning, harvesting, or other operations.
  • Environmental Control: Monitor weather conditions closely, particularly during periods of high humidity and warm temperatures.
