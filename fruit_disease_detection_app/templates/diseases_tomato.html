Tomato Leaf Diseases


Learn about various Tomato leaf diseases that can affect your crop. Know about the symptoms, root causes, favorable conditions and measures to prevent the disease.


1. Bacterial Spot

Disease symptoms:
  • Leaf yellowing and eventual defoliation, often starting with the lower leaves.
  • Small, water-soaked spots on leaves, which turn dark and become necrotic.

Root Causes:
  • Caused by the bacterium Xanthomonas campestris pv. vesicatoria.
  • Spread through contaminated seeds, transplants, or splashing water.

Favourable Conditions:
  • Warm, humid environments with temperatures between 75-86째F (24-30째C).
  • Frequent rainfall or overhead irrigation, which facilitates the spread of bacteria.

Preventive Measures:
  • Avoid overhead watering and improve air circulation by spacing plants appropriately.
  • Use disease-free seeds and transplants, and practice crop rotation.

2. Early Blight

Disease symptoms:

  • Dark, concentric spots on leaves, often with yellow halos.
  • Premature leaf drop and lesions on stems and fruit.

Root Causes:
  • Caused by the fungus Alternaria solani.
  • Spread through infected plant debris and seeds.

Favourable Conditions:
  • Warm, humid weather.
  • Poorly drained soils and overcrowded plants.

Preventive Measures:
  • Rotate crops and avoid planting tomatoes in the same spot each year.
  • Use resistant tomato varieties and practice proper spacing to ensure good air circulation.

3. Late Blight

Disease symptoms:
  • White, fuzzy growth on the underside of leaves under humid conditions.
  • Dark, water-soaked lesions on leaves, which can rapidly enlarge and cause leaf collapse.

Root Causes:
  • Caused by the pathogen Phytophthora infestans.
  • Spreads through infected plant material, soil, and water.

Favourable Conditions:
  • High humidity and prolonged leaf wetness.
  • Cool, wet weather conditions.

Preventive Measures:
  • Use resistant tomato varieties.
  • Apply fungicides and ensure proper spacing between plants to improve air circulation.

4. Leaf Mold

Disease symptoms:
  • Yellow spots on the upper leaf surfaces.
  • Olive-green to brown mold on the undersides of the leaves.

Root Causes:
  • Caused by the fungus Passalora fulva (formerly Cladosporium fulvum).
  • Spreads through air-borne spores and infected plant debris.

Favourable Conditions:
  • High humidity and temperatures between 22-24째C (72-75째F).
  • Poor ventilation and overcrowded plants.

Preventive Measures:
  • Use resistant varieties and apply appropriate fungicides.
  • Ensure good air circulation by proper plant spacing and pruning.

5. Septoria leaf spot

Disease symptoms:
  • Small, circular spots with dark brown margins and grayish centers on leaves.
  • Yellowing and premature dropping of infected leaves.

Root Causes:
  • Caused by the fungus Septoria lycopersici.
  • Spread through splashing water, contaminated tools, and infected plant debris.

Favourable Conditions:
  • Overhead irrigation that wets the foliage.

Preventive Measures:
  • Remove and destroy infected plant debris and practice crop rotation.

6. Two-Spotted Spider Mite

Disease symptoms:
  • Tiny, yellow or white spots on leaves, leading to a stippled or mottled appearance.
  • Fine webbing on the undersides of leaves and between plant parts.

Root Causes:
  • Infestation by Tetranychus urticae (two-spotted spider mite).

Favourable Conditions:
  • Hot, dry weather.
  • Dusty conditions that stress plants.

Preventive Measures:
  • Introduce natural predators like ladybugs or predatory mites.

7. Target spot

Disease symptoms:

  • Small, dark brown lesions with concentric rings, giving a target-like appearance.
  • Lesions may enlarge, causing leaf yellowing and defoliation.

Root Causes:
  • Caused by the fungus Corynespora cassiicola.
  • Spread through splashing water, contaminated tools, and infected plant debris.

Favourable Conditions:
  • Warm, humid weather.
  • Overhead irrigation that wets the foliage.

Preventive Measures:
  • Use drip irrigation to minimize leaf wetness.
  • Remove and destroy infected plant debris and apply appropriate fungicides.

8. Mosaic Virus

Disease symptoms:
  • Mottled light and dark green patterns on leaves, often with a blistered or wrinkled appearance.
  • Stunted growth and reduced fruit yield and quality.

Root Causes:
  • Caused by viruses such as Tobacco Mosaic Virus (TMV) and Tomato Mosaic Virus (ToMV).
  • Spread through contaminated tools, hands, infected seeds, and plant debris.

Favourable Conditions:
  • Mechanical transmission via handling, pruning, or planting.

Preventive Measures:
  • Use virus-resistant tomato varieties.
  • Practice good sanitation: disinfect tools, wash hands after handling plants, and avoid tobacco use around plants.

9. Yellow Leaf Curl Virus

Disease symptoms:
  • Stunted plant growth and reduced fruit production.
  • Upward curling and yellowing of leaf margins.

Root Causes:
  • Caused by Tomato Yellow Leaf Curl Virus (TYLCV).
  • Transmitted by the whitefly (Bemisia tabaci).

Favourable Conditions:
  • Warm temperatures favor whitefly proliferation.
  • High populations of whiteflies in and around the garden.

Preventive Measures:
  • Implement insect control measures, such as using yellow sticky traps and introducing natural predators like ladybugs.
